# **Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness Case Study**



### **Phishing Attack Case Study**



##### **Description of the Crime** 

###### Phishing is a fraudulent practice where attackers send deceptive communications—usually emails or text messages—that appear to come from a reputable source. The goal is to steal sensitive data like login credentials, credit card numbers, or personal identification.



##### **How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)**

**1.  The Bait: An attacker sends an urgent email that looks like an official communication from a university department or a bank.**

**2.  The Hook: The message claims there is a "security breach" or an "expired password" and provides a link to "fix" the issue.**

**3.  The Deception: The link leads to a fake login page that is a near-perfect replica of the real website.**

**4.  The Theft: The victim enters their username and password, which are then instantly captured by the attacker.**

**5.  The Exploit: The attacker uses these credentials to access the victim’s actual accounts, leading to data theft or financial loss.**



##### **Target Audience**

**College students are often targeted because they frequently use multiple digital platforms for education and financial transactions.**



##### **Consequences**

**Victims may face unauthorized financial transactions, loss of access to academic accounts, and long-term identity theft.**
